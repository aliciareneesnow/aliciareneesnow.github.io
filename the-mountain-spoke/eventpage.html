Cycle The Continental Divide Mark your calendar for June 23rd and join in the annual Cycle the Continental Divide. This event will follow The Great Divide Mountain Bike Route (GDMBR), crisscrossing the Continental Divide north to south. This route is defined by the word "remote." Its remoteness equates with spectacular terrain and scenery. The entire route is basically dirt-road and mountain-pass riding every day. In total, it has over 200,000 feet of elevation gain. Roughly 80% of it is composed of county, Forest Service (USFS), and Bureau of Land Management (BLM) dirt and gravel roads. The remainder is made up of four-wheel-drive tracks and singletrack trails (10%), and paved roads (10%).

Costs: $200 per person ($300 if bike rental needed)

Requirements: Must be over 16 years old with a recent physical of health.
